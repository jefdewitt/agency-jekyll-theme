  • Before 2007

    My Humble Beginnings

    Growing up around grief and mourning in the funeral business, I was steeped in empathy and naturally curious about how to comfort others. Unfortunately, I also learned how to self-soothe in unhealthy ways; by numbing myself.

  • 1996-2016

    A Life of Addiction

    Addiction was a constant in my life since childhood. It was all around me. After decades of my own struggle and years of failed attempts to manage it, I finally found sobriety. But getting sober didn’t ‘fix’ me. It opened my eyes.

  • July 11, 2016

    My Last Drink

    I began unraveling the knot of my addiction. I wanted to know how and why I became who I became and resorted to the things I did. I started consuming all the mental health material I could get my hands on, looking for help, looking for strategies, looking for answers.

  • March 2018

    Therapy

    The most consequential decision of my life was admitting that I needed help and then actually going out and finding it. But I haven't just attended therapy sessions, I worked therapy like it was giving me the cheat code to living the life I wanted.

  • March 2021

    Relationships

    A broken heart can be transformative. I channelled the energy I felt from a particularly disastrous relationship to face my fears of rejection and shame by going public with my thoughts and feelings.

  • Today

    My Content

    I endeavor to share all that I've learned in an effort to help others. It's a full circle back to my initial childhood curiosity to provide comfort in the ways I wish I had known it.
