My Story

Connecting the dots.

  • 1996-2016

    A Life of Addiction

    Addiction was a constant. After decades of struggle and years of failed attempts to manage it, I became sober. But getting sober didn’t ‘fix’ me. It opened my eyes.

  • July 11, 2016

    My Last Drink

    I wanted to know how and why I became who I was. I read everything I could find on breaking patterns, making lasting change, and treating psychological wounds. Eventually, I found therapy, the most consequential decision of my life.

  • March 2018

    Therapy

    I worked therapy like a job. I studied my life, my thoughts, my habits. I squared up to my pain. I found healing in sharing openly about my struggle with addiction and mental health. Soon, my focus progressed to relationships.

  • March 2021

    Relationships

    I went to relationship school. I read the works of experts like the Gottman's and Brene Brown. I learned the art of crafting dating profiles that lead to more meaningful connections. And point blank, I strove to be the kind of person I would want to build a relationship with.

  • Today

    Connectionist (just made it up)

    I write from experience, from my heart, from my pain. I'm not a therapist or a coach. I write because it heals. I write because it connects. I write what I needed to read when I needed it most.
