My Story

  • 1996-2016

    A Life of Addiction

    Addiction was a constant in my life. After decades of my own struggle and years of failed attempts to manage it, I finally found sobriety. But getting sober didn’t ‘fix’ me. It opened my eyes.

  • July 11, 2016

    My Last Drink

    I wanted to know how and why I became who I was. I read everything I could find on familiarizing myself with and healing my psychological wounds. Eventually I found therapy, the most consequential decision of my life.

  • March 2018

    Therapy

    I worked therapy like a job. I studied my life, my thoughts, my habits. I squared up to my pain. I found healing in sharing openly about my struggle with addiction and mental health. Soon, my focus progressed to relationships.

  • March 2021

    Relationships

    I needed relationship skills to be I who I wanted and attract who I wanted to be in relationships with. I learned incremental vulnerability to determine who was safe to open to. I discerned good matches. I learned interval validation > external validation.

  • Today

    My Own Guinea Pig

    I write from experience, from my heart, from my pain. I'm not a therapist or a coach. I write because it heals. I write because it connects. I write what I wish I had read when I needed it most.
